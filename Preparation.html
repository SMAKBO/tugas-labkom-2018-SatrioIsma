PREPARATION OF GLYCEROL

Glycerol is an alcohol sugar used in soaps and moisturizing lotions because of its high hygroscopic content (easily absorbs water from the air). Glycerol can also be used to preserve fruit and scientific specimens in biological research. Glycerol is also useful in fungi lubrication, baking, candy and printing ink, and prevents hydraulic jack from freezing. Although it can be made from vegetable oil, the most common method of glycerol-making is from animal fats. Look at step 1 below to create your own glycerol.

Equipment and Materials You Need:

1.1,7 kilogram fat

2.340 gram alkali

3.1 liter water

4.Salt

5.Stainless steel pan

6.Wood Spoon

7.Thermometer

8.Container

9.Small filter

10.Spoon filter

The Steps:

1.Prepare animal fat to be used. Although any type of animal fats may be used, cow's fat is the most commonly used type. Throw out all the skin, muscles, ligaments, tendons, and flesh to get the fat alone or so-called tallow.

2.Liquid the fat. Cut fat into small pieces and melt with low heat. Stir as needed.

3.Prepare an alkaline solution. Pour the alkali into the water slowly. Be careful in holding the container because the addition of alkali into the water will produce heat. Stir the solution slowly.

4.Cool the fat. Once thawed, remove the pan filled with fat from the stove and stir.

5.Make sure that the ingredients are ready for mixing. To make proper mixing, the fat and alkaline solution should have a temperature of about 35 degrees Celsius.

6.Combine fat and alkaline solution. Pour the alkaline solution slowly into the fat and stir it continuously.

7.Add salt. Pour salt into the mixture and continue to stir. Add salt to the thickened syrup scab formed on its surface (with liquid beneath it). If the scum already exists, stop stirring.

8.Lift the syrup. After the mixture cools and forms a texture that can be lifted from the pan with a filter spoon, lift the syrup layer. The remaining liquid in the pan is glycerol.
